📍 WHO'S MOVING & BUYING

GROWTH IS HOMEGROWN, NOT CALIFORNIAN

Out-of-state arrivals get the headlines, but the data says otherwise: California accounts for just 3% of tracked moves, and 74% of first-time buyers never left Texas. The real divide isn't where movers come from — it's the $33k income gap between who moves into new fringe housing and who inherits the historic core, and the 32.6% of households already doubled up because no smaller legal unit exists for them to move into.

🏗️ WHAT'S GETTING BUILT, AND WHERE

NEWER DOESN'T MEAN BIGGER

New construction has pulled back 32% in El Paso's fastest-growing zip codes, and it's landing on lots packed tighter, not larger. The mechanism that's supposed to send older homes down-market as buyers move to new construction is stalling: permits are down 45% since the 2010s, and the new-vs-resale price gap keeps widening.
